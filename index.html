ATR-Based Stop-Loss Guide

How ATR Stops Work

Formula:
Stop Distance = ATR(14) × Multiplier

Long: Stop = Anchor − (ATR × mult)
Short: Stop = Anchor + (ATR × mult)

Where Anchor = recent swing low/high or key support/resistance — not just the entry price.

Multipliers by regime:
1.0–1.5× — Low vol / choppy market
1.5–2.0× — Normal trending market ✓ (your default)
2.0–2.5× — High vol / earnings / news
2.5–3.0× — Extreme events (FOMC, CPI)

Then size the position so:
Dollar Risk = Account × Risk% = Qty × Stop Distance

Why Your Stops Are Getting Hit

Your avg loss hold time is 3.3h vs 1.6h for winners — you're holding losers twice as long.

Root cause: Fixed 5% stops placed at arbitrary % from entry — not anchored to structure. This means:
— Stops too tight on low-vol days → random stop-out
— Stops too wide on high-vol days → losses larger than intended

Fix with ATR:
1. Find ATR(14) on daily chart
2. Place stop at swing low/high
3. Confirm that distance ≤ 2× ATR
4. If wider → skip the trade
5. Size so stop distance = your dollar risk cap
